## Connecting Slack to SuperClaw

To securely connect your Slack workspace without manually juggling tokens, SuperClaw supports Slack OAuth.

### 1. Create a Slack App
1. Go to [api.slack.com/apps](https://api.slack.com/apps).
2. Click **Create New App** and choose **From scratch**.
3. Name your app (e.g., "SuperClaw") and select the workspace you wish to test in.

### 2. Get the Client ID and Secret
Because Slack uses OAuth, SuperClaw acts as an "Add to Slack" button. You won't have to copy tokens into the SuperClaw UI. Instead, SuperClaw itself needs the global App IDs.

1. In your Slack app settings, go to **Basic Information**.
2. Scroll down to **App Credentials**.
3. Here you will find the **Client ID** and **Client Secret**.
*(Note: As the SuperClaw administrator, you configure these globally in your server environment variables, enabling the one-click Connect button for your users.)*

### 3. Manual Token (For Self-Hosted / Internal Apps)
If SuperClaw is running in "Manual Fallback" mode, you'll need the tokens:
1. Go to **OAuth & Permissions** in the sidebar.
2. Under **Scopes**, add `chat:write`, `app_mentions:read`, and `channels:history` to the Bot Token Scopes.
3. Scroll back up and click **Install to Workspace**.
4. Once installed, copy the **Bot User OAuth Token** (`xoxb-...`).
5. Go back to **Basic Information** and copy the **Signing Secret**.

Paste the **Bot Token** and **Signing Secret** into SuperClaw!
